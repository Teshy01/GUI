Pokemon Diamond

One of the best generations of Pokemon of all time is generation four. The starter Pokemon in this generation had some of the coolest designs of all time, and the storyline was extremely interesting. The gameplay in this generation was also incredible, as difficulty was amped up and trainers would find themselves stuck between a rock and a hard place. As a child, I enjoyed playing through these games, and I put in a ton of effort to finish the game, post-game content, and the pokedex. Finishing the whole game and having all the pokemons allowed you to brag to your friends about how many hours you put into this brilliant game. For me, the start of this game was one of my favorite parts, since this game had, in my opinion, the best three starters of all time. Chimchar, Piplup and Turtwig were all brilliant starter Pokemon, and I loved each and every one of them. This webpage is dedicated to giving you a rundown on each of the starters, and help you admire each of them as well!


Chimchar

Chimchar is my favorite pokemon in this generation. He is the fire starter of this generation, and he is based off monkeys. He later evolves into a fighting fire type menace, which is extremely good late game. Below is a table of all the moves Chimchar learns by level up:

As you can see, the moveset is not really that great. That's why most people use TMs on Chimchar to make him insanely good. One of the best TMs you can teach Chimchar is flamethrower (you can learn this by level up as well, but it takes a long time to learn this move), and the move works beautifully with him if he has good special attack. Another fun moveset to run on him is all the punching moves, such as Thunder Punch, Fire Punch, Ice Punch and Mach Punch. If your Chimchar is a physical attacker, this moveset works brilliantly, as he has moves that could counter his weakness(such as flying and grass types). Piplup

Piplup, one of the cutest generation four pokemon, is probably my second favorite starter pokemon from this generation. It is the starting water type of this generation, and it has an awesome final evolution typing of water and steel. This pokemon was based of a penguin, and its definitely one of the best pokemon in this generation due to the lack of water types. Here is Piplup's full level up moveset:

Turtwig

Turtwig, the cutest of the three starters, is probably the most versatile of all of the starters in this generation. This starting grass type pokemon becomes a ground type after evolving, and it utterly demolishes ninety percent of the gyms in the league. It definitely is the best starter pokemon out of the three.
